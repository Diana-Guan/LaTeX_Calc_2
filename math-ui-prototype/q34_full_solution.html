Question 34

Evaluate \( \int \frac{x^2}{(4-x^2)^{3/2}}\,dx \).

  1. \(\displaystyle \frac{2x}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} - 2\sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \frac{x}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} - \sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{4}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} - 2\sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C\)
  4. I do not know

Solution: b) \(\displaystyle \frac{x}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} - \sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C\)

This question is asking you to: Compute an antiderivative by rewriting the denominator into a form that matches a standard trigonometric substitution pattern, then substitute back to write the final answer in terms of \(x\).
Step 1: Rewrite the denominator \((4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}\).
Step 1.1: Rewrite fractional exponents
\[\displaystyle a^{\frac{m}{n}}=\sqrt[n]{a^m}=\left(\sqrt[n]{a}\right)^m,\qquad \sqrt{a}=a^{\frac{1}{2}}.\]
Step 1.2: Rewrite the power \(\frac{3}{2}\)
Rewrite \(\frac{3}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\cdot 3\)
\((4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}\)
\(=\)
\((4-x^2)^{\frac{1}{2}\cdot 3}\)
Use \(a^{bc}=(a^b)^c\)
\((4-x^2)^{\frac{1}{2}\cdot 3}\)
\(=\)
\(\left((4-x^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}\right)^3\)
\(=\)
\(\left(\sqrt{4-x^2}\right)^3\)
Step 2: Set up trigonometric substitution.
Step 2.1: Define symbols

\(a\): constant, \(x\): unknown variable, \(\theta\): angle.

Step 2.2: Choose the substitution

From Step 1, the denominator contains \(\sqrt{4-x^2}\), which matches the pattern \(\sqrt{a^2-x^2}\). For this specific pattern, use the trig substitution \(x=a\sin\theta\).

Step 2.3: Identify \(a\) and \(x\)
\(4-x^2\)
\(=\)
\(2^2-x^2\)
\(a\)
\(=\)
\(2\)
\(x\)
\(=\)
\(a\sin\theta\)
\(=\)
\(2\sin\theta\)
Step 3: Rewrite the numerator \(x^2\).
Step 3.1: Square both sides
\(x\)
\(=\)
\(2\sin\theta\)
\(x^2\)
\(=\)
\((2\sin\theta)^2\)
Step 3.2: Apply \((ab)^2=a^2b^2\)
\(a\)
\(=\)
\(2\)
\(b\)
\(=\)
\(\sin\theta\)
\((2\sin\theta)^2\)
\(=\)
\(2^2(\sin\theta)^2\)
Step 3.3: Simplify
\(2^2\)
\(=\)
\(4\)
\((\sin\theta)^2\)
\(=\)
\(\sin^2\theta\)
\(2^2(\sin\theta)^2\)
\(=\)
\(4\sin^2\theta\)
Step 4: Rewrite \(4-x^2\) in terms of \(\theta\).
Step 4.1: Substitute \(x^2=4\sin^2\theta\)
\(4-x^2\)
\(=\)
\(4-4\sin^2\theta\)
Step 4.2: Factor out 4
Rewrite \(4=4\cdot 1\) and factor out \(4\)
\(4-4\sin^2\theta\)
\(=\)
\(4(1)-4(\sin^2\theta)\)
\(=\)
\(4(1-\sin^2\theta)\)
Step 4.3: Use \(1-\sin^2\theta=\cos^2\theta\)
Use the trigonometric identity and multiply both sides by \(4\)
\(1-\sin^2\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\cos^2\theta\)
Plug in \(x^2=4\sin^2\theta\)
\(4(1-\sin^2\theta)\)
\(=\)
\(4(\cos^2\theta)\)
\(4-x^2\)
\(=\)
\(4\cos^2\theta\)
Step 5: Compute \((4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}\) after substitution.
Step 5.1: Substitute into \(\left(\sqrt{4-x^2}\right)^3\)
Substitute \(4-x^2=4\cos^2\theta\)
\((4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}\)
\(=\)
\(\left(\sqrt{4-x^2}\right)^3\)
\(\left(\sqrt{4-x^2}\right)^3\)
\(=\)
\(\left(\sqrt{4\cos^2\theta}\right)^3\)
Step 5.2: Use \(\sqrt{ab}=\sqrt a\sqrt b\)
\(a\)
\(=\)
\(4\)
\(b\)
\(=\)
\(\cos^2\theta\)
Use the rule \((ab)^{1/2}=a^{1/2}b^{1/2}\)
\(\sqrt{4\cos^2\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(\sqrt4\,\sqrt{\cos^2\theta}\)
Step 5.3: Simplify \(\sqrt4\)
\(\sqrt4\)
\(=\)
\(2\)
\(\sqrt4\,\sqrt{\cos^2\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(2\sqrt{\cos^2\theta}\)
Step 5.4: Use \(\sqrt{u^2}=|u|\)
\(u\)
\(=\)
\(\cos\theta\)
\(\sqrt{\cos^2\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(|\cos\theta|\)
\(2\sqrt{\cos^2\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(2|\cos\theta|\)
\(\sqrt{4\cos^2\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(2|\cos\theta|\)
Step 5.5: Cube the result
\(\left(\sqrt{4\cos^2\theta}\right)^3\)
\(=\)
\((2|\cos\theta|)^3\)
\(=\)
\(2^3|\cos\theta|^3\)
\(=\)
\(8|\cos\theta|^3\)
Step 5.6: Remove the absolute value
\(0 \le \theta \le \frac{\pi}{2}\)
\(\Rightarrow\)
\(\cos\theta \ge 0\)
Since \(\cos\theta \ge 0\)
\(|\cos\theta|\)
\(=\)
\(\cos\theta\)
\(8|\cos\theta|^3\)
\(=\)
\(8\cos^3\theta\)
\((4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}\)
\(=\)
\(8\cos^3\theta\)
Step 6: Compute \(dx\).
Step 6.1: Differentiate \(x=2\sin\theta\)
\(x\)
\(=\)
\(2\sin\theta\)
Differentiate both sides with respect to \(\theta\)
\(\frac{dx}{d\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{d}{d\theta}(2\sin\theta)\)
Use the constant multiple rule
\(=\)
\(2\frac{d}{d\theta}(\sin\theta)\)
\(=\)
\(2\cos\theta\)
Step 6.2: Write \(dx\)
\(\frac{dx}{d\theta}\)
\(=\)
\(2\cos\theta\)
\(d\theta\left(\frac{dx}{d\theta}\right)\)
\(=\)
\((2\cos\theta)d\theta\)
\(dx\)
\(=\)
\(2\cos\theta\,d\theta\)
Step 7: Substitute into the integral.
Step 7.1: List substitutions
\(x^2\)
\(=\)
\(4\sin^2\theta\)
\((4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}\)
\(=\)
\(8\cos^3\theta\)
\(dx\)
\(=\)
\(2\cos\theta\,d\theta\)
Step 7.2: Substitute
\(\int \frac{x^2}{(4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}}\,dx\)
\(=\)
\(\int \frac{4\sin^2\theta}{8\cos^3\theta}\,(2\cos\theta\,d\theta)\)
Step 8: Simplify the integral.
Step 8.1: Multiply constants
\(\int \frac{4\sin^2\theta}{8\cos^3\theta}\,2\cos\theta\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \frac{4\cdot 2\sin^2\theta\cos\theta}{8\cos^3\theta}\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \frac{8\sin^2\theta\cos\theta}{8\cos^3\theta}\,d\theta\)
Step 8.2: Cancel 8
\(\int \frac{8\sin^2\theta\cos\theta}{8\cos^3\theta}\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \frac{\sin^2\theta\cos\theta}{\cos^3\theta}\,d\theta\)
Step 8.3: Cancel \(\cos\theta\)
\(\cos^3\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\cos^2\theta\cos\theta\)
\(\int \frac{\sin^2\theta\cos\theta}{\cos^3\theta}\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \frac{\sin^2\theta\cos\theta}{\cos^2\theta\cos\theta}\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \frac{\sin^2\theta}{\cos^2\theta}\,d\theta\)
Step 9: Rewrite using tangent.
Step 9.1: Use the definition of tangent
\(\tan\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\)
\(\tan^2\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{\sin^2\theta}{\cos^2\theta}\)
\(\int \frac{\sin^2\theta}{\cos^2\theta}\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \tan^2\theta\,d\theta\)
Step 10: Integrate in \(\theta\).
Step 10.1: Use \(\tan^2\theta=\sec^2\theta-1\)
\(\tan^2\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\sec^2\theta - 1\)
\(\int \tan^2\theta\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int (\sec^2\theta - 1)\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\int \sec^2\theta\,d\theta - \int 1\,d\theta\)
Step 10.2: Integrate
\(\int \sec^2\theta\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\tan\theta\)
\(\int 1\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\theta\)
\(\int \tan^2\theta\,d\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\tan\theta - \theta + C\)
Step 11: Convert back to \(x\).
Step 11.1: Find \(\theta\) using \(\sin^{-1}\)
\(x\)
\(=\)
\(2\sin\theta\)
\(\frac{1}{2}x\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{1}{2}2\sin\theta\)
\(\frac{x}{2}\)
\(=\)
\(\sin\theta\)
\(\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\)
Step 11.2: Find \(\tan\theta\)
\(\sin\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{x}{2}\)
\(\text{opposite}\)
\(=\)
\(x,\quad \text{hypotenuse} = 2\)
\(\text{adjacent}^2\)
\(=\)
\(2^2-x^2\)
\(=\)
\(4-x^2\)
\(\text{adjacent}\)
\(=\)
\(\sqrt{4-x^2}\)
\(\tan\theta\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{x}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}\)
Step 12: Write the final answer and choose the correct option.
Step 12.1: Substitute back
\(\int \frac{x^2}{(4-x^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}}\,dx\)
\(=\)
\(\tan\theta - \theta + C\)
\(=\)
\(\frac{x}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} - \sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C\)
Step 12.2: Final answer

The correct option is b.
