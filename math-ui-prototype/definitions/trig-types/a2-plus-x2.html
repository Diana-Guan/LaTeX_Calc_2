Pattern: \(\sqrt{a^2+x^2}\)

Definition

For \(\sqrt{a^2+x^2}\), use substitution \(x=a\tan\theta\).

Then \(\sqrt{a^2+x^2}=a\sec\theta\) (on the standard interval \(-\frac{\pi}{2}<\theta<\frac{\pi}{2}\)).

Simple Example

\(\sqrt{4+x^2}\):

\(a=2,\quad x=2\tan\theta\)

\(\sqrt{4+x^2}=\sqrt{2^2+x^2}=2\sec\theta\)

Proof

If \(x=a\tan\theta\), then

\[ \sqrt{a^2+x^2} =\sqrt{a^2+a^2\tan^2\theta} =a\sqrt{1+\tan^2\theta} =a\sqrt{\sec^2\theta} =a|\sec\theta|. \]

On the usual trig-substitution interval \(-\frac{\pi}{2}<\theta<\frac{\pi}{2}\), \(\sec\theta>0\), so this becomes \(a\sec\theta\).
